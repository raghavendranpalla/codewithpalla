Step 1 · Basics

ATS tip: use a simple professional email, and write your city as "City, Country" — job portals filter by location. Skip the photo for strict ATS portals; recruiters in India rarely expect one.

Step 2 · Professional summary

ATS tip: 3–4 lines with your role, years of experience and the top keywords from the job description (e.g. "Playwright", "API testing"). ATS software scores your resume by matching those exact words.

Step 3 · Skills

ATS tip: mirror the exact words from the job posting — write "Playwright", not "PW"; "API Testing", not "backend checks". Only list skills you can defend in the interview.

Step 4 · Work experience

ATS tip: start each bullet with an action verb and add a number — "Cut regression time from 3 days to 4 hours". Most recent role first. Freshers: add your course or internship project here as a role.

Step 5 · Projects

Education

ATS tip: projects are a fresher's experience — include a GitHub link. Keep degree names standard (B.Tech, B.E., MCA) so parsers recognise them.

Step 6 · Extras

ATS tip: only list certifications you can show a certificate for. Keep interests to one short line — they're a conversation starter, not a scoring factor.
